Zhenhan (Xander) PENG

I am currently a Ph.D. researcher at Centre for Industrial Management / Traffic and Infrastructure (CIB) , KU Leuven under the supervision of Prof. Johan Joubert. Meanwhile, I also work as a Part-time Research Assistant at The TIP Research Group, Dept. LSGI, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University (PolyU). Before pursuing my Ph.D. at KU Leuven, I received my master's degree in Geomatics from PolyU and bachelor's degree in Land Resources Management from Southwest University, respectively.

My research interests include:

  • Collaborative urban freight modelling
  • Large-scale micro-simulation
  • Agent-based modelling (primarily MATSim)
  • EV charging infrastructure optimization
  • Transport accessibility and equity
  • Spatial big data analysis

I am passionate about applying data-driven and micro-simulation approaches to address real-world transportation challenges, aiming to provide insights into urban logistics, smart mobility, and electrification of transportation.

Research Interests

Spatial Analysis and visualization

  • Explore patterns and relationships in spatial data using both statistical and spatial analysis methods
  • Reveal real-world problems and gain insights into complex spatial phenomena

Large-scale micro simulation

  • Model complex systems at an individual level
  • Simulate the individual behavior and further explore the potential impacts and interventions on complex systems

Urban Freight Modelling

  • Game-theory and optimization of urban freight system
  • Developing behaviourally sensitive activity- and agent-based model to assess the collaboration between freight operators and customers

Fuel options for electric mobility

  • Investigate which fuel option (e.g., Hydrogen, electricity, etc.) is more feasible for the electrification of transportation
  • Systematic analysis from multi-perspectives(e.g., economic, level of service, environmental, etc.)
